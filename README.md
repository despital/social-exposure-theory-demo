# Social Exposure Theory Experiment

A behavioral experiment investigating approach-avoidance behavior and social learning in group contexts.

## About the Experiment

This online experiment examines how people learn about and make decisions involving members of different social groups. The study consists of three phases:

1. **Phase 1: Social Learning** - Learn about individuals through repeated interactions and feedback
2. **Phase 2: Partner Choice** - Make decisions involving novel group members
3. **Phase 3: Post-Task Rating** - Evaluate all individuals encountered during the experiment

**Duration**: Approximately 20-25 minutes

## Experiment Structure

- Built with [jsPsych 8.2](https://www.jspsych.org/)
- Web-based, runs in any modern browser
- Data collected via Firebase Realtime Database
- Includes informed consent and demographics survey
- Debriefing provided at completion

### Study Conditions

The experiment includes different experimental conditions assigned via URL parameters:

- **Equal exposure conditions**: Balanced exposure to both groups
- **Majority exposure conditions**: Unequal exposure across groups
- **Informed/Uninformed**: With or without explicit group membership information
